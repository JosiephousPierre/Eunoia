How ORCA Works

A simple process that transforms psychological assessment in your institution

1

Assessment Initiation

Counselors or HR personnel initiate assessments for individuals or groups, selecting the appropriate Ryff Scale version (42, 54, or 84 items).

2

Adaptive Questionnaire

Participants complete the digital assessment with progress tracking and adaptive features that minimize survey fatigue while maintaining validity.

3

Automated Analysis

ORCA processes responses using advanced algorithms and Compositional Data Analysis to generate nuanced well-being profiles.

4

Professional Review

Counselors review AI-generated insights and recommendations before they're shared with participants, ensuring professional oversight.

5

Personalized Feedback

Participants receive tailored feedback on their psychological well-being across all six Ryff dimensions with actionable recommendations.

Secure & Private

Role-based access control and data encryption ensure sensitive psychological information remains protected and confidential. ORCA is designed with privacy at its core.

About ORCA

ORCA (Optimized Ryff-scale Computation & Analysis) was developed by a team of researchers and developers from the University of the Immaculate Conception to address the growing need for efficient, accurate psychological well-being assessment in educational settings.

Built on Carol Ryff's validated six-dimensional model of psychological well-being, ORCA transforms traditional assessment methods through automation, AI-powered interpretation, and user-centered design.
